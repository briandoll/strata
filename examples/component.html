Let readers choose their depth

Attention spans are shrinking, and AI summarization is one tap away. A lot of readers never reach the end of an article — they skim, or they paste it into a chatbot and read whatever comes back. That summary is unreviewed, and it isn't yours.

Strata offers a different deal. You, the publisher, write the full piece by hand. With an assistant's help you draft a one-sentence headline and a one-paragraph summary, then you read them, fix them, and approve them. All three ship together inside the page.

The reader lands on the full article. If they're in a hurry they zoom out to the summary, or all the way to the single sentence. If the sentence hooks them, they zoom back in. The words are always yours — Strata only changes which layer is showing.
